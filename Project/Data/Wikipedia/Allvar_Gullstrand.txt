He applied the methods of physical mathematics to the study of optical images and of the refraction of light in the eye. For this work, he received the [[Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine]] in 1911.\n\nGullstrand is noted also for his research on [[Astigmatism (eye)|astigmatism]]<ref>Gullstrand, Allvar (1890) ''Skand. Arch. f. Physiol.''; and (1901) ''Arch. f.  Ophth.'', 53, pp. 2, 185.</ref> and for improving the [[ophthalmoscope]] and corrective lenses for use after removal of a [[cataract]] from the eye.\n\nHe was elected a member of the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences]] in 1905, and served on the Academy's Prize Committee for Physics. While serving on the committee, he used his position to block [[Albert Einstein]] from receiving a [[Nobel Prize in Physics]] for his [[theory of relativity]], which Allvar believed to be wrong.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Gullstrand, Einstein, and the Nobel Prize |first=James G. |last=Ravin |journal=Arch Ophthalmol |year=1999 |volume=117 |issue=5 |pages=670\u2013672 |doi=10.1001/archopht.117.5.670|pmid=10326967 |doi-access=free }}</ref>\n\nGullstrand married Signe Breitholtz (1862\u20131946) in 1885.\n\nDue to the mathematical content of Gullstand's writing, [[Horatio Burt Williams]] lamented in 1926 that \"there are few ophthalmologists and not many physiologists who are able to read [Gullstrand's papers] and for the same reason that the chemists of [[Josiah Willard Gibbs|Gibbs']] day were unable to read his work.\"<ref>[[Horatio Burt Williams]] (1927) [https://projecteuclid.org/euclid.bams/1183492099 Mathematics and the Biological Sciences], [[Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society]] 33(3): 273\u201394 via [[Project Euclid]], quote from page 278</ref>\n\nGullstrand died in Stockholm in 1930, where he was interred at [[Norra begravningsplatsen]].\n\n==Works==\n* 1904: \"Zur Kenntnis der Kreispunkte\", [[Acta Mathematica]] 29:59\u2013100.\n* 1906: \"Vie r\u00e9elle optische Abbildung\", ''Kungl.
