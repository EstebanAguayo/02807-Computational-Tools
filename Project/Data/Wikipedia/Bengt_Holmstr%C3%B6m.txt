Samuelson Professor of Economics (Emeritus) at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]]. Together with [[Oliver Hart (economist)|Oliver Hart]], he received the Central Bank of Sweden [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences]] in 2016.<ref>{{cite news | last = Appelbaum | first = Binyamin | title = Oliver Hart and Bengt Holmstrom Win Nobel in Economics for Work on Contracts | newspaper = New York Times | date = 10 October 2016 | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/11/business/nobel-economics-oliver-hart-bengt-holmstrom.html | access-date = 10 October 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and education==\nHolmstr\u00f6m was born in [[Helsinki, Finland]] on 18 April 1949, and belongs to the [[Finland-Swedes|Swedish speaking minority]] of Finland.<ref name=Nobel_2016>{{cite news |title=Bengt Holmstr\u00f6m |url=https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economics/2016/holmstrom/facts/ |work=Nobel Prize }}</ref> He received his [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] in mathematics and science from the [[University of Helsinki]] in 1972.<ref name=Brittanica>{{cite encyclopedia |title=Bengt Holmstr\u00f6m |author=Brian Duignan |url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Bengt-Holmstrom |encyclopedia=[[Encyclopaedia Britannica]] }}</ref> He also received a [[Master of Science]] degree in [[Operations Research]] from [[Stanford University]] in 1975.\n\nHe received his [[Ph.D.]] from the [[Stanford Graduate School of Business|Graduate School of Business]] at Stanford in 1978.<ref name=Nobel_2016/> He moved to the United States in 1976.<ref name=DW_2016>{{cite news |title=Bengt Holmstr\u00f6m - the deal making authority |url=https://www.dw.com/en/bengt-holmstr\u00f6m-the-deal-making-authority/a-36710341 |work=[[Deutsche Welle|DW]] }}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nHe worked as a corporate planner from 1972 until 1974, then was an assistant professor at the [[Swedish School of Economics and Business Administration]] from 1978 until 1979.<ref name=Brittanica/> He served as an associate professor at the [[Kellogg Graduate School of Management]] at [[Northwestern University]] (1979\u20131983)<ref name=Brittanica/> and as the Edwin J. Beinecke Professor of Management at [[Yale University]]\u2019s School of Management (1983\u20131994). Holmstr\u00f6m was elected Alumnus of The Year by the University of Helsinki Alumni Association in 2010.\n\nHe has been on the faculty of M.I.T. since 1994, when he was appointed professor of economics and management at the department of economics and Sloan School of Management.<ref name=Brittanica/>\n\nHolmstr\u00f6m is particularly well known for his work on [[principal-agent theory]]. His work made seminal advances in understanding contracting in the presence of uncertainty.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2016|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2016/press-release/|access-date=13 November 2021|website=NobelPrize.org|language=en-US}}</ref> More generally, he has worked on the theory of contracting and incentives especially as applied to the [[theory of the firm]], to corporate governance and to liquidity problems in financial crises. He praised the taxpayer-backed [[Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008|bailouts by the US government]] during the [[financial crisis of 2007\u20132008]] and emphasizes the benefits of opacity in the [[money market]].<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Holmstr\u00f6m|first1=Bengt|title=Understanding the role of debt in the financial system|journal=BIS Working Papers|date=2015|issue=479|ssrn=2552018}}</ref>\n\nHolmstr\u00f6m was elected member of the [[Finnish Society of Sciences and Letters]] in 1992 and an honorary member of the same society in 2016. He is a fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]], the [[Econometric Society]], the [[European Economic Association]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fellows {{!}} EEA|url=https://www.eeassoc.org/fellows|access-date=23 March 2021|website=www.eeassoc.org}}</ref> and the [[American Finance Association]], and a foreign member of the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences]] and the [[Finnish Academy of Science and Letters]]. In 2011, he served as President of the Econometric Society. He holds honorary doctorate degrees from the [[Stockholm School of Economics]], Sweden, the [[University of Vaasa]] and the [[Hanken School of Economics]] in Finland.\n\nHolmstr\u00f6m was a member of [[Nokia]]'s [[board of directors]] from 1999 until 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nokia.com/global/about-nokia/about-us/governance/board/meet-the-board/|title=The latest Nokia phones and accessories &#124; Nokia Phones US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.engadget.com/2012/01/26/nokia-plans-board-of-directors-refresh-chairman-to-step-down/ |title=Nokia plans board of directors refresh, chairman to step down |publisher=Engadget.com |access-date=10 October 2016}}</ref> He is a member of the Board of the [[Aalto University]].<ref>Short [http://econ-www.mit.edu/faculty/bengt/short biography], [http://econ-www.mit.edu/files/5727 CV], and [http://econ-www.mit.edu/faculty/bengt/publications publications] from MIT.</ref><ref name=DW_2016/>\n\n==Accolades==\nHe was awarded the 2012 Banque de France-TSE Senior Prize in Monetary Economics and Finance, the 2013 Stephen A. Ross Prize in Financial Economics and the 2013 Chicago Mercantile Exchange \u2013 MSRI Prize for Innovative Quantitative Applications.\n\nIn 2016, Holmstr\u00f6m won the [[Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel]] together with [[Oliver Hart (economist)|Oliver Hart]] \"for their contributions to [[contract theory]]\".<ref name=\"nobel\">{{cite web|title=Press Release: The Prize in Economic Sciences 2016|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/economic-sciences/laureates/2016/press.html|publisher=The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences|access-date=10 October 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Personal life==\nHe is married to Anneli Holmstr\u00f6m<ref name=Court_2016>{{cite news |title=The King's dinner for the Nobel Laureates |url=https://www.kungahuset.se/royalcourt/latestnews/2016/2016/thekingsdinnerforthenobellaureates.5.47989bf9158b41184f9a0a.html |work=Swedish Royal Court |date=2016 }}</ref> with one son.<ref name=Nobel_2016/>\n\n==Publications==\n*Holmstr\u00f6m, Bengt, 1972. \"En icke-linear l\u00f6sningsmetod f\u00f6r allokationsproblem\".
