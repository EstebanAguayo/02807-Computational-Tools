He is the School Professor of Economics & Political Science and [[Regius Professor]] of Economics at the [[London School of Economics]], and Professor of European Studies at the [[University of Cyprus]]. His research focuses on topics of [[macroeconomics]], notably [[Labour (economics)|labour]], economic growth, and [[economic policy]]. In 2010, he was awarded the [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences|Nobel Prize in Economics]], jointly with [[Peter A. Diamond]] and [[Dale T. contributions==\n{{essay-like|section|date=February 2018}}{{peacock|section|date=February 2018}}\nPissarides is mostly known for his contributions to the [[search and matching theory]] for studying the interactions between the labour market and the [[macroeconomics|macro economy]]. He helped develop the concept of the [[Matching theory (macroeconomics)#The matching function|matching function]] (explaining the flows from unemployment to employment at a given moment of time), and pioneered the empirical work on its estimation. Pissarides has also done research on [[structural change]] and growth.\n\nOne of his most well-known papers is \"Job Creation and Job Destruction in the Theory of Unemployment\" (with [[Dale Mortensen]]), published in the ''[[Review of Economic Studies]]'' in 1994.<ref>{{cite journal |first1=Dale T. |last1=Mortensen |first2=Christopher A. |last2=Pissarides |year=1994 |title=Job Creation and Job Destruction in the Theory of Unemployment |url=http://www.iab.de/UserFiles/File/downloads/gradab/Dokumente%20Garloff/Mortensen_Pissarides_1994_Job%20creation%20and%20job%20destruction%20in%20the%20theory%20of%20unemployment_RES_pp_397_415.pdf|journal=[[Review of Economic Studies]] |volume=61 |issue=3 |pages=397\u2013415 |doi=10.2307/2297896 |jstor=2297896}}</ref> The paper was built on the previous individual contributions that both authors had made over the previous two decades.\n\nThe Mortensen\u2013Pissarides model that resulted from the paper has been exceptionally influential in modern [[macroeconomics]]. In one or another of its extensions or variations, it is now part of the core of most graduate economics curricula throughout the world.\n\nPissarides's book ''Equilibrium Unemployment Theory'', a standard reference in the literature of the macroeconomics of unemployment, is now in its second edition and was revised after his joint work with Mortensen resulted in the analysis of both endogenous [[job creation]] and destruction.\n\n==Awards and honours==\n* Fellow of the [[Econometric Society]], 1997<ref name=lsecv/>\n* [[Fellow of the British Academy]], 2002<ref name=lsecv/>\n* Fellow of the [[European Economic Association]], 2005\n* [[IZA Prize in Labor Economics]], jointly with [[Dale Mortensen]], 2005<ref>{{cite web|author=IZA |url=http://www.iza.org/en/webcontent/prize/history/prize2005/iza_prize |title=Prize |publisher=IZA |date=12 August 2010 |access-date=11 October 2010}}</ref>\n* Foreign Honorary Member of the [[American Economic Association]], 2011\n* Vice-president of the [[European Economic Association]], President in 2011\n* [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences|Nobel Prize in Economics]] in 2010, jointly with Dale Mortensen, [[Peter A.
