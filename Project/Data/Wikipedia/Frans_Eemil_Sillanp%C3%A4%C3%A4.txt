Although his parents were poor, they managed to send him to school in [[Tampere]]. At school Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 was a good student and with aid from his benefactor Henrik Liljeroos he entered the [[University of Helsinki]] in 1908 to study medicine.<ref name=\"AC\" /><ref name=\"bio\" /> Here his acquaintances included the painters [[Eero J\u00e4rnefelt]] and [[Pekka Halonen]], composer [[Jean Sibelius]] and author [[Juhani Aho]].<ref name=\"AC\">{{cite web |url= |access-date=2016-04-29}}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nFive years later, in 1913 Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 moved from Helsinki to his old home village and devoted himself to writing.<ref>[http://global.britannica.com/biography/Frans-Eemil-Sillanpaa Frans Eemil Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4] Encyclop\u00e6dia Britannica</ref> In 1914 Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 wrote articles for the newspaper ''[[Uusi Suometar]]''.<ref name=\"AC\"/>  In 1916 Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 married Sigrid Maria Salom\u00e4ki, whom he had met in 1914.<ref name=\"AC\" />\n\nBy principle, Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 was against all forms of violence and believed in scientific optimism.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.saunalahti.fi/panu/kirj1.htm | title = Frans Emil Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4n sotavuodet | author = Rajala, Panu | access-date = 2017-11-11}}</ref> In his work he portrayed rural people as living united with the land.<ref name=\"AC\"/>\n\nThe novel ''Hurskas kurjuus'' (Meek Heritage) (1919) depicted the reasons for [[Finnish Civil War]], and despite its objectivity, was controversial at the time.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://yle.fi/aihe/artikkeli/2017/01/03/frans-emil-sillanpaa-hurskas-kurjuus-miksi-paadyimme-sisallissotaan | title = Frans Emil Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4: Hurskas kurjuus \u2013 Miksi p\u00e4\u00e4dyimme sis\u00e4llissotaan? | website = Yle | date = 2017-01-03 | access-date = 2017-11-11}}</ref>\n\nSillanp\u00e4\u00e4 won international fame for his novel ''Nuorena nukkunut'' (translated to English as ''The Maid Silja'') in 1931.\n\nIn 1939, he was awarded the [[Nobel Prize in Literature]] \"for his deep understanding of his country's peasantry and the exquisite art with which he has portrayed their way of life and their relationship with Nature.\"<ref name=\"NP\">{{cite web |url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/literature/laureates/1939/sillanpaa-bio.html |title=Frans Eemil Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 \u2013 Biographical |date= 2014 |website=Nobelprize.org |publisher=Nobel Media AB |access-date=2016-04-29 }}</ref> A few days after he received the prize, talks between Finland and Soviet Union broke down and the [[Winter War]] began.<ref name=\"bio\" /><ref name=\"fes\" /> Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 donated the golden medal to be melted for funds to aid the war effort.<ref name=\"fes\">{{cite web | url = http://www.fesillanpaanseura.org/nobel-palkinto | title = Nobel palkinto | website = fesillanpaanseura.org | access-date = 2017-11-11}}</ref>\n\nBefore the Winter War, Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 wrote the lyrics for what is known as ''[[Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4n marssilaulu]]'' to lift his spirits when his eldest son Esko was partaking in military practices at [[Karelian Isthmus]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.tunturisusi.com/sotilasmarssit/sillanpaanmarssilaulu.htm | title= Sota-ajan musiikkia \u2013 Maailman Matista Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4n Marssilauluun | access-date=2017-11-10}}</ref>\n\nIn 1939 his wife Sigrid died of [[pneumonia]] leaving eight children with Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4.<ref name=\"bio\">{{cite web | url = https://kansallisbiografia.fi/kansallisbiografia/henkilo/700 | title = Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4, Frans Emil (1888\u20131964) | website = Biografiakeskus | access-date = 2017-11-11}}</ref> Some time after, Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 married his secretary Anna von Hertzen (1900\u20131983) and traveled to Stockholm to receive the Nobel prize.<ref name=\"bio\" />\n\nIn 1941 Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 divorced his wife Anna. His alcoholism and other ailments needed hospital treatment. In 1943 he returned to public life as a bearded old 'Grandpa Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4'. His radio appearances, especially his tradition of talking on Christmas Eve from 1945 to 1963 became very popular.<ref name=\"KB\">{{cite web |url=http://www.kansallisbiografia.fi/english/?id=700 |title=Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4, Frans Emil (1888\u20131964) |website=The National Biography of Finland |publisher=SKS |access-date=2016-04-29 }}</ref>\n\nThe [[asteroid]] [[1446 Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4]], discovered on January 26, 1938 by the renowned Finnish astronomer and physicist [[Yrj\u00f6 V\u00e4is\u00e4l\u00e4]], was named after him.\n\n==Death==\nSillanp\u00e4\u00e4 died on 3 June 1964 in Helsinki aged 75.\n\n==Works==\n[[File:Mauno-Oittinen-1931.jpg|thumb|right|Sillanp\u00e4\u00e4 sitting for the sculptor Mauno Oittinen in 1931.]]\n* ''El\u00e4m\u00e4 ja aurinko'' (1916)\n* ''Ihmislapsia el\u00e4m\u00e4n saatossa'' (1917)\n* ''[[Hurskas kurjuus]]'' (translated as ''Meek Heritage'') (1919)\n* ''Rakas is\u00e4nmaani'' (1919)\n* ''Hiltu ja Ragnar'' (1923)\n* ''Enkelten suojatit'' (1923)\n* ''Omistani ja omilleni'' (1924)\n* ''Maan tasalta'' (1924)\n* ''T\u00f6llinm\u00e4ki'' (1925)\n* ''Rippi'' (1928)\n* ''Kiitos hetkist\u00e4, Herra...''
