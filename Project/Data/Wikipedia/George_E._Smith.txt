He was awarded a one-quarter share in the 2009 [[Nobel Prize in Physics]] for \"the invention of an imaging semiconductor circuit\u2014the CCD sensor, which has become an electronic eye in almost all areas of photography\".<ref>{{citation | title = The Nobel Prize in Physics 2009 | url = http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/2009/index.html | publisher = Nobel Foundation | date = 2009-10-06 | access-date = 2009-10-06}}.</ref>\n\n==Early life==\nSmith was born in [[White Plains, New York|White Plains]], [[New York (state)|New York]].  Smith served in the US Navy, and subsequently obtained his [[Bachelor of Science|B.Sc.]] degree from the [[University of Pennsylvania]] in 1955 and his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] degree from the [[University of Chicago]] in 1959 with a dissertation of only eight pages.<ref name=\"proquest\">{{citation | title = THE ANOMALOUS SKIN EFFECT IN BISMUTH | publisher = University of Chicago | date = 1959-12-06 | id = {{ProQuest|301893432}} }}</ref> \n\n==Career==\nHe worked at [[Bell Labs]] in [[Murray Hill, New Jersey]] from 1959 to his retirement in 1986, where he led research into novel lasers and semiconductor devices.  During his tenure, Smith was awarded dozens of patents and eventually headed the [[VLSI]] device department.<ref name=\"Earthtimes\">{{citation | title = PROFILE: George Smith - Nobel winner and world sailor | url = http://www.earthtimes.org/articles/show/288984,profile-george-smith--nobel-winner-and-world-sailor.html | publisher = EarthTimes | date = 2009-10-06 | access-date = 2009-10-06}}.</ref>\n\nIn 1969, Smith and [[Willard Boyle]] invented the [[Charge-Coupled Device]] (CCD),<ref name=\"Nobelpressrelease\">{{cite book|last=Smith|first=George E.|title=Les Prix Nobel. Smith|editor=Karl Grandin|access-date=22 May 2012}}</ref>  for which they have jointly received the [[Franklin Institute]]'s [[Stuart Ballantine Medal]] in 1973, the 1974 [[IEEE Morris N. Liebmann Memorial Award]], the 2006 [[Charles Stark Draper Prize]], and the 2009 [[Nobel Prize in Physics]].\n\nBoth Boyle and Smith were avid sailors who took many trips together.  After retirement Smith sailed around the world with his life partner, Janet, for seventeen years, eventually giving up his hobby in 2003 to \"spare his 'creaky bones' from further storms\".<ref name=\"Earthtimes\" /> He currently resides in the [[Waretown, New Jersey|Waretown]] section of [[Ocean Township, Ocean County, New Jersey]].<ref>Staff.
