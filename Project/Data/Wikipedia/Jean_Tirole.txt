He focuses on [[industrial organization]], [[game theory]], banking and finance, and [[psychological economics|economics and psychology]]. In 2014 he was awarded the [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences]] for his analysis of [[market power]] and [[regulation]].<ref>{{citation |title=Sveriges Riksbank's Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2014. |url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/economic-sciences/laureates/2014/ |publisher=Sveriges Riksbank |date=October 13, 2014 |access-date=October 13, 2014}}</ref><ref>Biography of [http://www.ecgi.org/members_directory/member.php?member_id=80 Jean Tirole] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141018173655/http://www.ecgi.org/members_directory/member.php?member_id=80 |date=18 October 2014 }}</ref>\n\n== Education ==\nTirole received engineering degrees from the [[\u00c9cole Polytechnique]] in Paris in 1976, and from the [[\u00c9cole nationale des ponts et chauss\u00e9es]] in 1978. He graduated as a member of the elite [[Corps of Bridges, Waters and Forests (France)|Corps of Bridges, Waters and Forests]].  Tirole pursued graduate studies at the [[Paris Dauphine University]] and was awarded a [[Master of Advanced Studies|DEA]] degree in 1976 and a [[Doctorat#France|Doctorat de troisi\u00e8me cycle]] in [[decision theory|decision mathematics]] in 1978.  In 1981, he received a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in economics from the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] for his thesis titled ''Essays in economic theory'', under the supervision of [[Eric Maskin]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://library.mit.edu/item/000117200 | title = Essays in economic theory / by Jean Tirole | publisher = MIT Library catalog | access-date = 2014-10-13}}</ref>\n\n== Career ==\n{{Expand section|date=October 2014}}\n[[File:(31) - Toulouse School of Economics.jpg|thumb|180px|left|[[Toulouse School of Economics]].]]\nTirole is chairman of the board of the [[Jean-Jacques Laffont Foundation]] at the [[Toulouse School of Economics]], and scientific director of the [[Industrial Economics Institute]] (IDEI) at [[Toulouse 1 University Capitole]]. After receiving his doctorate from MIT in 1981, he worked as a researcher at the [[\u00c9cole nationale des ponts et chauss\u00e9es]] until 1984. From 1984\u20131991, he worked as Professor of Economics at [[MIT]]. His work by 1988 helped to define modern industrial organization theory by organising and synthesising the main results of the game-theory revolution ''vis-\u00e0-vis'' understanding of non-competitive markets.<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite journal|title = Jean Tirole: Market Power and Regulation|date = 13 October 2014|journal = Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences}}</ref>\n\nFrom 1994 to 1996 he was a professor of economics at the [[\u00c9cole Polytechnique]]. Tirole was involved with [[Jean-Jacques Laffont]] in the project of creating a new [[Toulouse School of Economics|School of Economics in Toulouse]]. He is Engineer General of the Corps of Bridges, Waters and Forest, serving as Chair of the Board of the [[Toulouse School of Economics]], Visiting Professor at MIT and Professor \"cumulant\" at the [[\u00c9cole des hautes \u00e9tudes en sciences sociales]] since 1995.\n\nHe was president of the [[Econometric Society]] in 1998 and of the [[European Economic Association]] in 2001. Around this time, he was able to determine a way to calculate the optimal prices for the regulation of natural monopolies and wrote a number of articles about the regulation of capital markets\u2014with a focus on the differential of control between decentralised lenders and the centralised control of bank management.<ref name=\":0\" /> Tirole has been a member of the [[Acad\u00e9mie des Sciences morales et politiques]] since 2011, the Conseil d'analyse \u00e9conomique since 2008 and the Conseil strat\u00e9gique de la recherch\u00e9 since 2013. In the early 2010s, he showed that banks generally tend to take short-term risks and recommended a change in [[quantitative easing]] towards a more quality-based market stimulation policy.<ref name=\":0\" />\n\n== Contributions to economics ==\nTirole's textbook, ''The Theory of Industrial Organization'', synthesised modern models of oligopolistic competition, analysing various cases where industries consist of a small number of firms with significant market power. He and [[Oliver Hart (economist)|Oliver Hart]] published a paper showing the conditions in which a vertical merger can result in foreclosure.<ref>{{Cite book|title=The theory of industrial organization|last=Tirole |first=Jean|date=1988|publisher=The MIT Press|isbn=9780262200714|oclc=464151427|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/theoryofindustri00jean}}</ref> Rochet and Tirole analysed the implications of 2-sided markets for competition policy.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Rochet|first1=Jean-Charles|last2=Tirole|first2=Jean|date=2003-06-01|title=Platform Competition in Two-Sided Markets|journal=Journal of the European Economic Association|volume=1|issue=4|pages=990\u20131029|doi=10.1162/154247603322493212|issn=1542-4766|doi-access=free}}</ref> [[Drew Fudenberg|Fudenberg]] and Tirole also created a taxonomy of strategic effects in oligopolistic competition models.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rasmusen.org/GI/reader/15a.aerfud&Tirole.Puppy.pdf|title=The Fat Cat Effect, the Puppy Dog Ploy and the Lean an d Hungry Look}}</ref>\n\n== Awards ==\nTirole was awarded the [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences]] in 2014 for his analysis of [[market power]] and the regulation of natural monopolies. Tirole received [[doctorate honoris causa|doctorates ''honoris causa'']] from the [[Universit\u00e9 libre de Bruxelles]] in 1989, the [[London Business School]] and the [[University of Montreal]] in 2007, the [[University of Mannheim]] in  2011, the [[Athens University of Economics and Business]] and the [[University of Rome Tor Vergata]] in 2012 as well as the [[University of Lausanne]] in 2013.<ref>{{in lang|it}} [http://www.economia.uniroma2.it/nuovo/eventi.asp?id=1078 Laurea magistrale honoris causa Jean Tirole]</ref>\n\nTirole also received the inaugural [[BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award]] in the Economics, Finance and Management category in 2008, the Public Utility Research Center Distinguished Service Award ([[University of Florida]]) in 1997, and the [[Yrj\u00f6 Jahnsson Award]] of the [[Yrj\u00f6 Jahnsson Foundation]] and the [[European Economic Association]] in 1993. He is a foreign honorary member of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] (1993) and of the [[American Economic Association]] (1993). He has also been a [[Sloan Fellow]] (1985) and a [[Guggenheim Fellow (1988)]]. He was a fellow of the Econometric Society in 1986 and an Economic Theory Fellow (Society for the Advancement of Economic Theory) in 2011. In 2013 Tirole was elected an Honorary Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Edinburgh]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rse.org.uk/fellow/jean-tirole/|title=Professor Jean Marcel Tirole HonFRSE - The Royal Society of Edinburgh|work=The Royal Society of Edinburgh|access-date=2018-02-01|language=en-GB}}</ref>\n\nIn 2007 he was awarded the highest award (the Gold Medal or m\u00e9daille d'or) of the French CNRS. In 2008, he received the Prix du Cercle d'Oc; in 2009, he received an  Outstanding Contributions to the Profession Award ([[International Association for Energy Economics]]); in 2010, he was granted the [[Chicago Mercantile Exchange]] \u2013 [[Mathematical Sciences Research Institute]] (CME-MSRI) prize in Innovative Quantitative Innovations in Finance,  the Tjalling Koopmans Asset Award ([[Tilburg University]]), and  the \"Prix Claude Levi-Strauss\". He is among the most influential economists in the world according to [[RePEc|IDEAS/RePEc]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ideas.repec.org/top/top.person.all.html|title=Economist Rankings at IDEAS|access-date=15 October 2014}}</ref>  Besides his numerous academic distinctions, he was the recipient of the Gold Medal of the city of Toulouse in 2007, a [[Chevalier de la L\u00e9gion d'honneur]] since 2007 and an Officer in the [[Ordre national du M\u00e9rite]] since 2010.\n\n== Publications ==\nTirole has published about 200 professional articles in economics and finance, as well as 10 books, including  ''The Theory of Industrial Organization'', ''Game Theory'' (with [[Drew Fudenberg]]), ''A Theory of Incentives in Procurement and Regulation'' (with [[Jean-Jacques Laffont]]), ''The Prudential Regulation of Banks'' (with Mathias Dewatripont), ''Competition in Telecommunications'' (with Jean-Jacques Laffont), ''Financial Crises, Liquidity, and the International Monetary System'', and  ''The Theory of Corporate Finance''. His research covers industrial organization, regulation, game theory, public economics, banking and finance, psychology and economics, international finance and macroeconomics.\n\n=== Books ===\n* ''Dynamic Models of Oligopoly'' (with D.
