<!--(filename only)-->\n|footnotes         =\n}}\n\n'''Louis Eug\u00e8ne F\u00e9lix N\u00e9el''' {{post-nominals|country=UK|ForMemRS}} (22 November 1904 \u2013 17 November 2000) was a French [[physicist]] born in [[Lyon]].<ref name=formemrs>{{cite journal|last=Friedel|first=J.|author2=Averbuch, P. |title=Louis Eug\u00e8ne F\u00e9lix N\u00e9el. 22 November 1904 \u2013 17 November 2000|journal=Biographical Memoirs of Fellows of the Royal Society|date=1 December 2003|volume=49|pages=367\u2013384|doi=10.1098/rsbm.2003.0021|s2cid=74311735}}</ref>\n\n==Biography==\nN\u00e9el studied at the [[Lyc\u00e9e du Parc]] in [[Lyon]] and was accepted at the [[\u00c9cole Normale Sup\u00e9rieure]] in [[Paris]]. He obtained the degree of Doctor of Science at the [[University of Strasbourg]]. He was corecipient (with the [[Sweden|Swedish]] [[astrophysicist]] [[Hannes Alfv\u00e9n]]) of the [[Nobel Prize]] for [[Physics]] in 1970 for his pioneering studies of the magnetic properties of [[solid]]s.<ref>{{cite journal|last=N\u00e9el|first=L.|title=Magnetism and Local Molecular Field|journal=Science|date=3 December 1971|volume=174|issue=4013|pages=985\u2013992|doi=10.1126/science.174.4013.985|pmid=17757022|bibcode = 1971Sci...174..985N |s2cid=27784885|url=https://semanticscholar.org/paper/7a9640a05337471b9366a129c1d7fb1d3d8b0f7f}}</ref> His contributions to [[solid state physics]] have found numerous useful applications, particularly in the development of improved [[computer]] memory units. About 1930 he suggested that a new form of magnetic behavior might exist; called [[antiferromagnetism]], as opposed to [[ferromagnetism]]. Above a certain temperature (the [[N\u00e9el temperature]]) this behaviour stops. N\u00e9el pointed out (1948)<ref>{{cite journal|last=N\u00e9el|first=Louis|title=Magnetic Properties of Ferrites: Ferrimagnetism and Antiferromagnetism|journal=Ann. Phys. (Paris)|date=1948|volume=3|pages=137\u2013198}}</ref> that materials could also exist showing [[ferrimagnetism]]. N\u00e9el has also given an explanation of the weak magnetism of certain rocks, making possible the study of the history of [[Earth's magnetic field]].<ref>{{cite journal|last=N\u00e9el|first=Louis|title=Some theoretical aspects of rock-magnetism|journal=Advances in Physics|date=1 April 1955|volume=4|issue=14|pages=191\u2013243|doi=10.1080/00018735500101204|bibcode = 1955AdPhy...4..191N |url=https://hal.archives-ouvertes.fr/hal-02888358/file/N%C3%A9el%20-%201955%20-%20Some%20theoretical%20aspects%20of%20rock-magnetism.pdf}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=Barbara|first1=B.|last2=Lacroix |first2=Claudine |title=Retrospective in Science: Louis Neel (1904\u20132000)|journal=Science|year=2000|volume=291|issue=5506|pages=1000|doi=10.1126/science.1059052|pmid=11232560|s2cid=161752562}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last=Coey|first=Michael|title=Obituary: Louis N\u00e9el (1904\u20132000) |journal=Nature|year=2001|volume=409|issue=6818|pages=302|doi=10.1038/35053274|bibcode=2001Natur.409..302C|s2cid=36961096|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last=Coey|first=J.
