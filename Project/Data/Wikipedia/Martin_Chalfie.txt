He is [[List of university professors at Columbia University|University Professor]] at [[Columbia University]].<ref>[http://www.newsdesk.se/pressroom/kungliga_vetenskapsakademien__kva/pressrelease/view/the-nobel-prize-in-chemistry-2008-243710 The Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2008] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081011113057/http://www.newsdesk.se/pressroom/kungliga_vetenskapsakademien__kva/pressrelease/view/the-nobel-prize-in-chemistry-2008-243710 |date=2008-10-11 }}</ref> He shared the 2008 [[Nobel Prize in Chemistry]] along with [[Osamu Shimomura]] and [[Roger Y. Tsien]] \"for the discovery and development of the [[green fluorescent protein]], GFP\".<ref>[http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2008/ Website of the Nobel Prize committee.]</ref> He holds a [[Ph.D.]] in [[neurobiology]] from [[Harvard University]].\n\n==Education and early life==\n[[File:Krugman-Tsien-Chalfie-Shimomura-Kobayashi-Masukawa-press conference Dec 07th, 2008-1.jpg|thumb|[[Paul Krugman]], [[Roger Tsien]], Martin Chalfie, [[Osamu Shimomura]], [[Makoto Kobayashi (physicist)|Makoto Kobayashi]] and [[Toshihide Masukawa]], Nobel Prize Laureates 2008, at a press conference at the [[Swedish Academy of Science]] in Stockholm.]]\nChalfie grew up in [[Chicago, Illinois]], son of the guitarist Eli Chalfie (1910\u20131996) and owner of an apparel store Vivian Chalfie (n\u00e9e Friedlen, 1913\u20132005). His maternal grandfather, Meyer L. Friedlen, immigrated to Chicago from [[Moscow]] at an early age; his paternal grandparents, Benjamin and Esther Chalfie, came to [[Cincinnati]] from [[Brest, Belarus|Brest-Litovsk]].<ref>[http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2008/chalfie.html?print=1 Autobiography on Nobelprize.org]</ref>\n\nHe matriculated at [[Harvard University]] in 1965, intending to be a math major,<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\">{{cite journal|title=Profile of Martin Chalfie|date=2008-01-28|publisher=[[United States National Academy of Sciences|National Academy of Sciences]]|first=Tinsley |last=Davis |journal=[[Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences]]|volume=105|issue=5|pages=1393\u20135|doi=10.1073/pnas.0704615105|pmid=18227519|pmc=2234153|bibcode=2008PNAS..105.1393D|doi-access=free}}</ref> but he switched to biochemistry because it combined his interests in chemistry, math, and biology.<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/>  He spent the summer after his junior year working in the laboratory of [[Klaus Weber]] at Harvard, but \"It was so disheartening to completely fail that I decided I shouldn't be in biology.\"<ref name=\"Wu2008_10_09\">{{cite news|newspaper=[[Harvard Crimson]]|title=Harvard Alumni Win Nobel Prize|first=June Q.|last=Wu|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article.aspx?ref=524525|date=2008-10-09}}</ref>  As a result, in his senior year, he completed his major and took courses in law, theater, and Russian literature.<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/>\n\nHe also competed on the swim team at Harvard and was named captain in his senior year. At the time, swimming coach Bill Brooks said, \"Marty will make an excellent captain because he has the admiration of the entire team.\"<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article.aspx?ref=139729|title=Chalfie is named tankers' captain|date=1968-03-21|newspaper=[[Harvard Crimson]]}}</ref>\nAs captain, he won the Harold S. Ulen trophy, awarded \"to a senior on the Harvard team who best demonstrates those qualities of leadership, sportsmanship, and team cooperation as exemplified by Harold S. Ulen.\"<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article.aspx?ref=494782|title=Two Awards Presented To Diver Bill Murphy|date=1969-04-18|first=Bennett H.|last=Beach|newspaper=[[Harvard Crimson]]}}</ref> Following the announcement of Chalfie's Nobel award, his freshman-year roommate observed of Chalfie, \"He would always identify himself as a swimmer.\"<ref name=\"Wu2008_10_09\"/>\n\nAfter graduating in 1969, he worked at a variety of temporary jobs, such as selling dresses for his parents' dress manufacturing business in Chicago <ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/> and teaching at [[Hamden Hall Country Day School]] in [[Hamden, Connecticut]].<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/>   In the summer of 1971, his research at the laboratory of [[Jose Zadunaisky]] at [[Yale University]] resulted in his first publication.  With revived confidence,<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/> he returned to Harvard for graduate studies under  [[Robert Perlman]], and received his [[Ph.D.]] in 1977.\n\n==Career and research==\nChalfie conducted his [[postdoctoral]] research at the [[Laboratory of Molecular Biology]] (LMB) with [[Sydney Brenner]] and [[John Sulston]], and the three published a paper in 1985 on \"The Neural Circuit for Touch Sensitivity in ''[[Caenorhabditis elegans|C. elegans]]''\".<ref>[http://www.jneurosci.org/cgi/reprint/5/4/956.pdf \"The Neural Circuit for Touch Sensitivity in ''C. Elegans''\"]</ref>  Chalfie then left the LMB in 1982 to join the faculty of [[Columbia University]] in the department of biological sciences and continued to study ''[[Caenorhabditis elegans|C. elegans]]'' touch mutants.\n\nHe married [[Tulle Hazelrigg]]. She later joined him on the faculty of [[Columbia University]].<ref name=\"Davis_2008_01_28\"/>  She gave him permission to cite her unpublished research in his seminal Science paper \"Green Fluorescent Protein as a Marker for Gene Expression\"<ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1126/science.8303295 | last1 = Chalife | first1 = M | last2 = Tu | first2 = Y| year = 1994 | last3 = Euskirchen | first3 = G | last4 = Ward | first4 = WW | last5 = Prasher | first5 = DC | title = Green Fluorescent Protein as a Marker for Gene Expression | url = https://semanticscholar.org/paper/e8b5c07fae8e094408383a6aafc529a054173bf4| journal = Science | volume = 263 | issue = 5148| pages = 802\u2013805 | pmid = 8303295 | bibcode = 1994Sci...263..802C | s2cid = 9043327 }}</ref> on condition that he made coffee, cooked, and emptied the garbage nightly for a month.<ref>[http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2008/chalfie-slides.pdf Nobel Lecture slide 17]</ref>\n\nChalfie and his wife had a daughter, Sarah, in July 1992.\n \nChalfie was elected to the [[United States National Academy of Sciences|National Academy of Sciences]] in 2004.\n\nHe slept through the phone call from the Nobel Prize Committee.<ref name=\"Wu2008_10_09\"/><ref name=\"Smith2008_10_08\"/> When he woke up, he knew the prize would have been announced already, so he said \"Okay, who's the [[schnook]] that got the Prize this time?\" And so he opened up his laptop, got to the Nobel Prize site and found out that he was the schnook!<ref name=\"Smith2008_10_08\">{{cite web|url=http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2008/chalfie-telephone.html|title= Interview with Martin Chalfie|publisher=[[Nobel Foundation]]|first=Adam|last=Smith}}</ref>\n\nIn 2015, Chalfie signed the ''[[Mainau Declaration|Mainau Declaration 2015 on Climate Change]]'' on the final day of the 65th [[Lindau Nobel Laureate Meetings|Lindau Nobel Laureate Meeting]]. The declaration was signed by a total of 76 Nobel Laureates and handed to then-President of the French Republic, [[Fran\u00e7ois Hollande]], as part of the successful [[Paris Climate Change Conference|COP21 climate summit]] in Paris.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mainaudeclaration.org/|title=Mainau Declaration|website=www.mainaudeclaration.org|access-date=2018-01-11}}</ref>\n\nChalfie's lab uses the [[nematode]] ''[[Caenorhabditis elegans|C. elegans]]'' to investigate aspects of nerve cell development and function. The wealth of developmental, anatomical, genetic, and molecular information available for ''C. elegans'' provides a powerful and multifaceted approach to these studies.\n\nHe has published over 100 papers of which at least 25 have over 100 citations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomsonreuters.com/products_services/science/science_products/a-z/web_of_science/|title=Web of Science|publisher=Thomson 2011}}</ref>\n\nHe traces his work on [[green fluorescent protein]] to a 1988 seminar from [[Paul Brehm]] about bioluminescent organisms, which led to some crucial experiments in 1992, detailed in his
