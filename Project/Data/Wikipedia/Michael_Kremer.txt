|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/uploads/2019/10/press-economicsciences2019.pdf |publisher=Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and education==\nMichael Robert Kremer was born in 1964 to Eugene and Sara Lillian (n\u00e9e Kimmel) Kremer in New York City. His father, Eugene Kremer was the son of Jewish immigrants to the US from Austria-Poland. His mother, Sara Lillian Kremer was a professor of English literature, who specialized in American Jewish and Holocaust literature.  Her parents were Jewish immigrants to the US from Poland.<ref>{{cite news | title = JEWISH NOBEL PRIZE WINNERS IN ECONOMICS | url = http://www.jinfo.org/Nobels_Economics.html}}</ref> He graduated from [[Harvard University]] (A.B. in Social Studies in 1985 and Ph.D. in economics in 1992).<ref name=\":01\"/><ref>{{cite news | last = Aggarwal-Schifellite | first = Manisha | title = Nobel in economic sciences awarded: Michael Kremer wins Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel | newspaper = The Harvard Gazette | date = October 14, 2019 | url = https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2019/10/harvards-michael-kremer-recognized-with-nobel-in-economics/}}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nA postdoctoral fellow at [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] (MIT) from 1992 to 1993, Kremer was a visiting assistant professor at the [[University of Chicago]] in Spring 1993, and professor at MIT from 1993 to 1999. From 1999 to 2020, he was a professor at [[Harvard University]]. He joined the faculty at the University of Chicago as a professor in the [[Kenneth C. Griffin]] Department of Economics, the college, and the [[Harris School of Public Policy]] on September 1, 2020.<ref name=\":01\"/><ref name=Chicago/>\n\nKremer has focused his research on [[poverty]] reduction, often as it relates to [[education economics]] and [[health economics]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.apnews.com/777efc95f30f41cc997ee47ce04c1c89 | title = 3 economists who study poverty win Nobel Prize | last1 = Wiseman | first1 = Paul | last2 = Ljubojevic | first2 = Aleksandar | last3 = LeBlanc | first3 = Steve | date = October 14, 2019 | website = apnews.com | publisher = Associated Press | quote = The three winners, who have worked together, revolutionized developmental economics by pioneering field experiments that generate practical insights into how poor people respond to education, health care and other programs meant to lift them out of poverty.}}</ref> Working with Abhijit Banerjee and Esther Duflo (with whom he shared the 2019 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics), he helped establish the effectiveness of [[randomized controlled trials]] to test proposed antipoverty measures.<ref>{{cite journal | last = Cho | first = Adrian | title = Economics Nobel honors trio taking an experimental approach to fighting poverty | journal = Science | date = October 14, 2019 | url = https://www.sciencemag.org/news/2019/10/economics-nobel-honors-trio-taking-experimental-agpproach-fighting-poverty| doi = 10.1126/science.aaz7975 | s2cid = 210377958 }}</ref> Describing Kremer's early use of pioneering experimental methods, Duflo said that Kremer \u201cwas there from the very beginning, and took enormous risks . . . . He is a visionary.\u201d<ref>{{cite news | last = Smialek | first = Jeanna | title = Nobel Economics Prize Goes to Pioneers in Reducing Poverty: Three professors, Abhijit Banerjee and Esther Duflo, both of M.I.T., and Michael Kremer of Harvard, were honored. | newspaper = The New York Times | date = October 14, 2019 | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2019/10/14/business/nobel-economics.html}}</ref>\n\nKremer is a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]],<ref>https://www.amacad.org/person/michael-kremer Retrieved July 25, 2020, Saturday</ref> a recipient of a MacArthur Fellowship (1997)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/fellows/556/|title=MacArthur Foundation|website=www.macfound.org|language=en|access-date=July 23, 2018}}</ref> and a Presidential Faculty Fellowship, and was named a Young Global Leader by the [[World Economic Forum]].<ref>https://www.weforum.org/agenda/authors/michael-kremer/ Retrieved July 25, 2020, Saturday</ref> He is a research affiliate at [[Innovations for Poverty Action]](IPA),<ref>https://www.poverty-action.org Retrieved July 25, 2020, Saturday</ref> a [[New Haven, Connecticut]]-based research outfit dedicated to creating and evaluating solutions to social and international development problems. Kremer is a member of [[Giving What We Can]], an [[effective altruism]] organization whose members pledge to give 10% of their income to effective charities.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.givingwhatwecan.org/about-us/members/|title=Members|website=www.givingwhatwecan.org}}</ref> He is founder and president of [[WorldTeach]], a Harvard-based organization which places college students and recent graduates as volunteer teachers on summer and year-long programs in developing countries around the world. He is also co-founder of [[:Draft:Precision Agriculture for Development|Precision Development (PxD)]], a non-profit organization that leverages the global emergence of the mobile phone to provide digital agronomic advisory services to [[smallholding|smallholder]] farmers at scale.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.precisionag.org|title=PAD|website=www.precisionag.org}}</ref>\n\nKremer started the advanced market commitment, which focuses on creating incentive mechanisms to encourage the development of [[vaccine]]s for use in developing countries, and the use of randomized trials to evaluate interventions in the social sciences.
