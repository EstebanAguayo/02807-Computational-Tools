approximately 500<ref name=NTI1/>\n| budget = \u20ac69.69&nbsp;million/year (2019)\n| website = {{url|www.opcw.org/}}\n}}\n\nThe '''Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons''' ('''OPCW''') is an [[intergovernmental organization|intergovernmental organisation]] and the implementing body for the [[Chemical Weapons Convention]] (CWC), which entered into force on 29 April 1997. The OPCW, with its 193 member states, has its seat in [[The Hague]], [[Netherlands]]; it oversees the global endeavour for the permanent and verifiable [[Destruction of chemical weapons|elimination of chemical weapons]].\n\nThe organisation promotes and verifies the adherence to the Chemical Weapons Convention, which prohibits the use of [[chemical weapons]] and requires their destruction. Verification consists both of evaluation of declarations by member states and onsite inspections.\n\nThe organisation was awarded the [[2013 Nobel Peace Prize]] \"for its extensive efforts to eliminate chemical weapons\". Nobel Committee chairman [[Thorbj\u00f8rn Jagland]] said, \"The conventions and the work of the OPCW have defined the use of chemical weapons as a [[taboo]] under [[international law]]\".\n\n==History==\n[[File:HQ of OPCW in The Hague.jpg|thumb|upright|The headquarters of the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons]]\n\nThe Hague was chosen as the location for the seat of the organisation after a successful lobby of the Dutch government, competing against [[Vienna]] and [[Geneva]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.denhaag.nl/en/residents/to/An-Expats-View-Peter-Kaiser.htm|publisher=city of [[The Hague]]|title=An Expat's View: Peter Kaiser|date=8 October 2009|access-date=1 November 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110928144246/http://www.denhaag.nl/en/residents/to/An-Expats-View-Peter-Kaiser.htm|archive-date=28 September 2011}}</ref> The organisation has its headquarters next to the [[World Forum Convention Centre]] (where it holds its yearly Conference of States Parties) and an equipment store and laboratory facility in [[Rijswijk]]. The principal body is the '''Conference of the States Parties (CSP)''', which normally is convened yearly, and in which all countries can participate, with equal voting rights. Countries are generally represented in the conference by a [[permanent representative]] to the organisation, which in most cases is also the ambassador to the Netherlands. The conference decides on all main topics regarding the organisation (for example, taking retaliation measures) and the convention (approving guidelines, imposing retaliating measures against members).<ref name=NTI1>{{cite web|url=http://www.nti.org/treaties-and-regimes/organization-for-the-prohibition-of-chemical-weapons/|website=Nuclear Threat Initiative - Treaties & Regimes|title=Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW)|access-date=11 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131012004623/http://www.nti.org/treaties-and-regimes/organization-for-the-prohibition-of-chemical-weapons/|archive-date=12 October 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>\n\nThe '''Executive Council (EC)''' is the executive organ of the organisation and consists of 41 states parties, which are appointed by the conference on a two-year term. The council amongst others oversees the budget and cooperates with the General Secretariat on all matters related to the convention.<ref name=NTI1/>\n\nThe '''Technical Secretariat (TS)''' applies most of the activities mandated by the council and is the body where most of the employees of the organisation work. The main activities of the OPCW are performed by the inspection and the verification divisions.\n\nAll states parties make contributions to the OPCW budget, based on a modified UN scale of assessments.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nti.org|title=Nuclear Threat Initiative \u2013 NTI|access-date=14 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110225213817/http://nti.org/|archive-date=25 February 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> The OPCW budget for 2020 is \u20ac70,958,760\n<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.opcw.org/media-centre/opcw-numbers|title=OPCW by the Numbers|website=OPCW|language=en|access-date=31 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190202115546/https://www.opcw.org/media-centre/opcw-numbers|archive-date=2 February 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>\n\n== Powers ==\nThe OPCW has the power to report on whether chemical weapons were used in an attack it has investigated.<ref name=bbcchem/>\n\n\"The OPCW has the power to send inspectors to any signatory country to search for evidence of production of banned chemicals. Conviction by the Administrative Tribunal of the International Labour Organization ==\nIn 2002, the United States convened an extraordinary session of the Conference of the States Parties of the OPCW to request the dismissal of [[Jos\u00e9 Bustani]], then Director General of the OPCW.<ref name = \" NYT131013 \">Marlise Simons, [https://www.nytimes.com/2013/10/14/world/to-ousted-boss-arms-watchdog-was-seen-as-an-obstacle-in-iraq.html To Ousted Boss, Arms Watchdog Was Seen as an Obstacle in Iraq], '' The New York Times, '' 13 October 2013.</ref> Bustani was dismissed following the vote, held on 22 April 2002, with 48 states voting in favor, 7 against and 43 abstaining.<ref name = \"NYT131013\" /><ref>[https://www.opcw.org/fileadmin/OPCW/CSP/C-SS-1/fr/C-SS-1_5-FR.pdf REPORT OF THE FIRST EXTRAORDINARY SESSION OF THE CONFERENCE OF STATES PARTIES] , '' OPCW, '' 22 July 2002.</ref> Subsequently, Bustani accused the United States of having provoked his impeachment because he had succeeded in convincing [[Saddam Hussein]] to ratify the [[Chemical Weapons Convention]], which implied inspection of the Iraqi arsenal by OPCW investigators and would have thwarted the American plan of an invasion of Iraq.<ref name = \"NYT131013\" /><ref>Ellen Barry, [https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/20/world/europe/russia-chemical-weapons-novichok.html Russia, Praised for Scrapping Chemical Weapons, Now Under Watchdog's Gaze], '' The New York Times '' , 20 March 2018.</ref> He also lodged a complaint before the [[Administrative Tribunal of the International Labour Organization]], which, by a judgment of 16 July 2003, quashed the dismissal and condemned the OPCW to compensation for material and moral damage.<ref>Site of the International Labor Organization, [http://www.ilo.org/dyn/triblex/triblexmain.fullText?p_lang=en&p_judgment_no=2232&p_language_code=EN judgment 2232].</ref> Bustani did not seek to be reinstated in office.\n\n==See also==\n*[[1990
