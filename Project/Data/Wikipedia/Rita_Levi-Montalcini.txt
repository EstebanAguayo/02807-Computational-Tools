research==\nLevi-Montalcini lost her assistant position in the anatomy department after a 1938 law barring Jews from university positions was passed.<ref>Yount, Lisa, ''Twentieth-Century Women Scientists'', Facts on File, Inc., 1996, p. 29, {{ISBN|0-8160-3173-8}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=May 2018}} During World War II she set up a laboratory in her bedroom in Turin and studied the growth of nerve fibers in chicken embryos, discovering that nerve cells die when they lack targets, and laying the groundwork for much of her later research.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Goldstein |first=Bob |date=2 December 2021 |title=A Lab of Her Own |url=https://nautil.us/a-lab-of-her-own-13306/ |magazine=Nautilus |access-date=19 December 2021}}</ref> She described this experience decades later in the science [[documentary film]] ''[[Death by Design/The Life and Times of Life and Times]]'' (1997).<ref>{{cite web|title=Death by Design: Where Parallel Worlds Meet|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0118947/fullcredits#cast|publisher=IMDb|access-date=31 December 2012}}</ref> The film also features her fraternal [[twin]] sister [[Paola Levi-Montalcini|Paola]], who became a respected artist best known for her aluminum sculptures designed to bring light to the rooms due to the reflective white surface.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Di Genova |first1=Giorgio |title=Paola Levi-Montalcini |url=https://jwa.org/encyclopedia/article/levi-montalcini-paola |website=Jewish Women's Archive}}</ref>\n\nWhen the Germans invaded Italy in September 1943, her family fled south to [[Florence, Italy|Florence]], where they survived the [[Holocaust]], under false identities, protected by some non-Jewish friends.<ref>\"[http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/rita-levi-montalcini_(Dizionario-Biografico)/ Rita Levi Montalcini]\", Treccani.it.</ref> During the Nazi occupation, Levi-Montalcini was in contact with the partisans of the [[Action Party (Italy)|Action Party]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ebri.it/en/pagine/1_biografia_2_biography_30|title = EBRI - European Brain Research Institute}}</ref> After the liberation of Florence in August 1944, she volunteered her medical expertise for the Allied health service. Her family returned to Turin in 1945.\n\nIn September 1946, Levi-Montalcini was granted a one-semester research fellowship in the laboratory of Professor [[Viktor Hamburger]] at [[Washington University in St. Louis]]; he was interested in two of the articles Levi-Montalcini had published in foreign scientific journals.<ref name=\"beckerexhibits.wustl.edu\">{{cite web |url=http://beckerexhibits.wustl.edu/mowihsp/bios/levi_montalcini.htm|title=Missouri Women in the Health Sciences - Biographies - Rita Levi-Montalcini}}</ref> After she duplicated the results of her home laboratory experiments, Hamburger offered her a research associate position, which she held for 30 years. It was there that, in 1952, she did her most important work: isolating [[nerve growth factor]] (NGF) from observations of certain cancerous tissues that cause extremely rapid growth of nerve cells.<ref name=\"fox1\">{{cite news|url=http://www.foxnews.com/world/2012/12/30/nobel-winning-scientist-levi-montalcini-dies-in-rome-at-103-biologist-studied/|title=Nobel-winning scientist Levi-Montalcini dies in Rome at 103, biologist studied growth factor|date=30 December 2012|publisher=Fox News Channel}}</ref> By transferring pieces of tumors to chick embryos, Montalcini established a mass of cells that was full of nerve fibers. The discovery of nerves growing everywhere like a halo around the tumor cells was surprising. When describing it, Montalcini said it is: \"like rivulets of water flowing steadily over a bed of stones.\"<ref name=makersmodernsci>{{cite book|last=Yount|first=Lisa|title=Rita Levi-Montalcini: Discoverer of Nerve Growth Factor|year=2009|publisher=Chelsea House}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=May 2018}} The nerve growth produced by the tumor was unlike anything she had seen before \u2013 the nerves took over areas that would become other tissues and even entered veins in the embryo. But nerves did not grow into the arteries, which would flow from the embryo back to the tumor. This suggested to Montalcini that the tumor itself was releasing a substance that was stimulating the growth of nerves.\n\nShe was made a full professor in 1958. In 1962, she established a second laboratory in [[Rome]] and divided her time between there and St. Louis. In 1963, she became the first woman to receive the Max Weinstein Award (given by the United Cerebral Palsy Association) due to her significant contributions into neurological research.<ref name=\"beckerexhibits.wustl.edu\"/>\n\nFrom 1961 to 1969, she directed the Research Center of Neurobiology of the [[Consiglio Nazionale delle Ricerche|CNR]] (Rome), and from 1969 to 1978, the Laboratory of Cellular Biology.<ref name=\"fox1\"/> After she retired in 1977, she was appointed as director of the Institute of Cell Biology of the Italian National Council of Research in Rome. She later retired from that position in 1979, however continued to be involved as a guest professor.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Wasserman|first1=Elga R.|title=The door in the dream : conversations with eminent women in science|date=2000|publisher=Joseph Henry Press|isbn=0-309-06568-2|page=[https://archive.org/details/doorindream00elga/page/41 41]|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/doorindream00elga/page/41}}</ref>\n\nLevi-Montalcini founded the European Brain Research Institute in 2002, and then served as its president.<ref name=eashingtonuni>{{cite web|title=Rita Levi-Montalcini|url=http://beckerexhibits.wustl.edu/mig/bios/levi-montalcini.html|publisher=Washington University|access-date=31 December 2012}}</ref><ref name=network>{{cite web|title=The European Brain Research Institute in Rome|url=http://www.eni-net.org/organization/home-institutions/the-european-brain-research-institute-in-rome/|publisher=Network of European Neuroscience Institutes|access-date=31 December 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120724141339/http://www.eni-net.org/organization/home-institutions/the-european-brain-research-institute-in-rome/|archive-date=24 July 2012}}</ref> Her role in this institute was at the center of some criticism from some parts of the scientific community in 2010.<ref>{{cite journal |doi=10.1038/463270a |pmid=20090705 |title=Self-inflicted damage.The autocratic actions of an institute's founder could destroy a centre of excellence for brain research |journal=Nature |volume=463 |pages=270 |date=21 January 2010 |issue=7279|bibcode=2010Natur.463..270. |doi-access=free }}</ref>\n\nControversies were raised about the cooperation of Levi-Montalcini with the Italian pharmaceutical concern Fidia. While working for Fidia, she improved the understanding of [[gangliosides]]. Beginning in 1975, she supported the drug Cronassial (a particular mixture of gangliosides) produced by Fidia from bovine [[brain]] tissue. Independent studies showed that the drug actually could be successful in treatment of intended diseases ([[peripheral neuropathies]]).<ref>{{cite book |author=Horowitz SH |title=Ganglioside Structure, Function, and Biomedical Potential |chapter=Ganglioside (Cronassial) therapy in diabetic neuropathy |volume=174 |pages=593\u2013600 |year=1984 |pmid=6377852 |doi=10.1007/978-1-4684-1200-0_50|series=Advances in Experimental Medicine and Biology |isbn=978-1-4684-1202-4 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |vauthors=Staughton RC, Good J |title=Double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial of a mixture of gangliosides ('Cronassial') in post-herpetic neuralgia |journal=Current Medical Research and Opinion |volume=12 |issue=3 |pages=169\u201376 |year=1990 |pmid=2272191 |doi=10.1185/03007999009111498}}</ref> Years later, some patients under treatment with Cronassial reported a severe neurological syndrome ([[Guillain\u2013Barr\u00e9 syndrome]]). As per the normal cautionary routine, Germany banned Cronassial in 1983, followed by other countries. Italy prohibited the drug only in 1993; at the same time, an investigation revealed that Fidia paid the Italian Ministry of Health for a quick approval of Cronassial and later paid for pushing use of the drug in treatment of diseases where it had not been tested.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dmi.unipg.it/~mamone/univ/QUALIT.htm|title=Qualit\u00e0 Intellettuale|publisher=UNIPG|access-date=16 March 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dica33.it/argomenti/farmacologia/farmacovigilanza/farmacovigilanza2.asp|title=Fallimenti storici|publisher=Dica33|access-date=16 March 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://politicamolecolare.blogspot.com/2007/11/rita-levi-di-montalcini-e-i.html|title=Rita Levi Montalcini e la vicenda Cronossial|publisher=Politica Molecolare|date=November 2011}}</ref> Levi-Montalcini's relationship with the company was revealed during the investigation, and she was criticized publicly.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1994/02/22/nobel-comprato-non-ne-so-nulla.html|title=Nobel comprato? Non ne so nulla|access-date=6 June 2010}}</ref>\n\nIn the 1990s, she was one of the first scientists pointing out the importance of the [[mast cell]] in human pathology.<ref name=Leon1994>{{cite journal  |vauthors=Leon A, Buriani A, Dal Toso R, etal |title=Mast cells synthesize, store, and release nerve growth factor |journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America |volume=91 |issue=9 |pages=3739\u201343 |date=April 1994  |pmid=8170980 |pmc=43657 |doi=10.1073/pnas.91.9.3739|bibcode=1994PNAS...91.3739L |doi-access=free }}</ref> In the same period (1993), she identified the endogenous compound [[palmitoylethanolamide]] as an important modulator of this cell.<ref name=Aloe1993>{{cite journal |vauthors=Aloe L, Leon A, Levi-Montalcini R |s2cid=20577242 |title=A proposed autacoid mechanism controlling mastocyte behaviour |journal=Agents and Actions |volume=39 Spec No |pages=C145\u20137 |year=1993 |pmid=7505999|doi=10.1007/BF01972748 }}</ref> Understanding this mechanism initiated a new era of research into this compound which has resulted in more discoveries regarding its mechanisms and benefits, a far better understanding of the [[endocannabinoid system]] and new [[Liposome|liposomal]] palmitoylethanolamide product formulations designed specifically for improved absorption and bioavailability.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Hesselink|first=Jan M Keppel|date=8 August 2013|title=Evolution in pharmacologic thinking around the natural analgesic palmitoylethanolamide: from nonspecific resistance to PPAR-\u03b1 agonist and effective nutraceutical|journal=Journal of Pain Research|volume=6|pages=625\u2013634|doi=10.2147/JPR.S48653|issn=1178-7090|pmc=3744360|pmid=23964161}}</ref>\n\nLevi-Montalcini earned a Nobel Prize along with Stanley Cohen in 1986 in the physiology or medicine category. The two earned their Nobel Prizes for their research in to the nerve growth factor (NGF), the protein that causes cell growth due to stimulated nerve tissue.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.famousscientists.org/rita-levi-montalcini/|title=Rita Levi-Montalcini - Biography, Facts and Pictures}}</ref>\n\n==Political career==\nOn 1 August 2001, she was appointed as [[Senator for Life]] by the [[President of Italy]], [[Carlo Azeglio Ciampi]].<ref name=Sen/>\n\nOn 28\u201329 April 2006, Levi-Montalcini, aged 97, attended the opening assembly of the newly elected Senate, at which the President of the Senate was elected. Due to her support of the government of [[Romano Prodi]], she was often criticized by some right-wing senators, who accused her of saving the government when the government's exiguous majority in the Senate was at risk. He was one of the best-known contemporary Italian [[architects]] and a professor at the [[University of Turin]]. She had two sisters: Anna, five years older than Rita, and [[Paola Levi-Montalcini|Paola]], her twin sister, a popular artist who died on 29 September 2000, age 91.\n\nIn 2003, she filed a libel suit for defamation against [[Beppe Grillo]]. During a show, Grillo called the 94-year-old woman an \"old whore\".<ref>{{cite web|author=Gian Marco Chiocci |url=http://www.ilgiornale.it/news/vaffa-e-condanne-camere-tab-grillo.html|title=Tra \"vaffa\" e condanne, Camere tab\u00f9 per Grillo|date=5 October 2007|language=it|publisher=[[Il Giornale]]|access-date=29 December 2012}}</ref>\n\nLevi-Montalcini never married and had no children.<ref name=\":0\" /> In a 2006 interview she said, \"I never had any hesitation or regrets in this sense... I have never felt lonely.\"<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2012/dec/30/rita-levi-montalcini-biologist-dies | title=Rita Levi-Montalcini, pioneering Italian biologist, dies at 103 | date=20 December 2012 | work=The Guardian| access-date=29 November 2016}}</ref> She died in her home in Rome on 30 December 2012 at the age of 103.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ansa.it/web/notizie/rubriche/scienza/2012/12/30/morta-Rita-Levi-Montalcini_8011133.html|title=Addio al premio Nobel Rita Levi Montalcini|publisher=ANSA|date=30 December 2012}}</ref>\n\nUpon her death, the Mayor of Rome, [[Gianni Alemanno]], stated it was a great loss \"for all of humanity.\" He praised her as someone who represented \"civic conscience, culture and the spirit of research of our time.\" Italian astrophysicist [[Margherita Hack]] told Sky TG24 TV in a tribute to her fellow scientist, \"She is really someone to be admired.\" Italy's premier, [[Mario Monti]], paid tribute to Levi-Montalcini's \"charismatic and tenacious\" character and for her lifelong endeavor to \"defend the battles in which she believed.\" Vatican spokesman [[Federico Lombardi]] praised Levi-Montalcini's civil and moral efforts, saying she was an \"inspiring\" example for Italy and the world.<ref name=nbc3012>{{cite news|last=D'Emilio|first=Frances|title=Nobel-winning biologist Rita Levi-Montalcini dies at 103|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/50324234|access-date=31 December 2012|publisher=NBC News|date=30 December 2012|agency=Associated Press}}</ref>\n\nAccording to the former President of the [[Grand Orient of Italy]], she was invited and participated in many cultural events organized by the main Italian Masonic organization.<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.repubblica.it/politica/2010/06/09/news/massoneria_pd-4683769/ | title = I massoni di sinistra. book|last=Wasserman|first=Elga|title=The Door in the Dream: Conversations With Eminent Women in Science|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Xyt3IAAtmEIC&pg=PT61|year=2000|publisher=[[Joseph Henry Press]]|isbn=0309086191|page=61}}</ref> elected to the [[United States National Academy of Sciences]].<ref name=\"2007-LYount\">{{cite book|last=Yount|first=Lisa|title=A to Z of Women in Science and Math|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=428i2UdWRRAC&pg=PA174|year=2007|publisher=[[Infobase Publishing]]|isbn=978-1438107950|page=174}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=May 2018}} She was elected an [[European Molecular Biology Organization|EMBO]] Member in 1974.<ref name=membo/>\n\nIn 1970, she received the Golden Plate Award of the [[Academy of Achievement|American Academy of Achievement]].<ref>{{cite web|title= Golden Plate Awardees of the American Academy of Achievement |website=achievement.org|publisher=[[American Academy of Achievement]]|url=https://achievement.org/our-history/golden-plate-awards/#science-exploration}}</ref>\n\nIn 1974, she became a member of the [[Pontifical Academy of Sciences]]<ref name=pas>{{cite web|title=Rita Levi-Montalcini|url=http://www.casinapioiv.va/content/accademia/en/academicians/ordinary/levimontalcini.html|publisher=The Pontifical Academy of Sciences|access-date=31 December 2012}}</ref>\n\nIn 1983, she was awarded the [[Louisa Gross Horwitz Prize]] from [[Columbia University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://embryo.asu.edu/view/embryo:123950|title=Rita Levi-Montalcini \u2013 The Embryo Project Encyclopedia|publisher=ASU|access-date=30 December 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110317221534/http://embryo.asu.edu/view/embryo:123950|archive-date=17 March 2011}}</ref>\n\nIn 1985, she was awarded the [[Ralph W. Gerard Prize in Neuroscience]].\n\nIn 1986, she was elected to the [[American Philosophical Society]].<ref>[https://search.amphilsoc.org/memhist/search?creator=MOntalcini&title=&subject=&subdiv=&mem=&year=&year-max=&dead=&keyword=&smode=advanced American Philosophical Society Member History (Dr. Rita Levi-Montalcini)]</ref>\n\nIn 1986, Levi-Montalcini and collaborator [[Stanley Cohen (biochemist)|Stanley Cohen]] received the [[Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine|Nobel Prize in Medicine]],<ref name=\"fox1\"/> as well as the [[Albert Lasker Award for Basic Medical Research]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laskerfoundation.org/awards/1986_b_description.htm#montalcini|title=Albert Lasker Basic Medical Research Award: 1986 Winners|publisher=[[Lasker Foundation]]|access-date=31 December 2012|archive-date=16 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130216115746/http://www.laskerfoundation.org/awards/1986_b_description.htm#montalcini|url-status=dead}}</ref> This made her the fourth Nobel Prize winner to come from Italy's small (less than 50,000 people) but very old Jewish community, after [[Emilio Segr\u00e8]], [[Salvador Luria]] (a university colleague and friend) and [[Franco Modigliani]].\n\nIn 1987, she received the [[National Medal of Science]], the highest American scientific honor.<ref name=\"2007-LYount\"/>\n\nIn 1991, she received the Laurea Honoris Causa in Medicine from the [[University of Trieste]], Italy. On that occasion, she expressed her desire to formulate a Carta of Human Duties as necessary counterpart of the too much neglected Declaration of Human Rights. The vision of Rita Levi-Montalcini came true with the issuing of the Trieste Declaration of Human Duties and the foundation in 1993 of the International Council of Human Duties, International Council of Human Duties (ICHD), at the University of Trieste.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www2.units.it/ichd/|title=International Council of Human Duties|access-date=2 January 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120611074639/http://www2.units.it/ichd/|archive-date=11 June 2012}}</ref>\n\nShe was elected a [[List of Fellows of the Royal Society elected in 1995|Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS) in 1995]].<ref name=formemrs>{{cite web|author=Anon|year=2015|title=Fellowship of the Royal Society 1660\u20132015|url=https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1RVVZY00MZNrK2YCTTzVrbTFH2t3RxoAZah128gQR-NM/pubhtml|publisher=[[Royal Society]]|location=London|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151015185820/https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1RVVZY00MZNrK2YCTTzVrbTFH2t3RxoAZah128gQR-NM/pubhtml|archive-date=15 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>\n\nIn 1999, Levi-Montalcini was nominated Goodwill Ambassador of the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization ([[FAO]]) by FAO Director-General Jacques Diouf.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet the Goodwill Ambassadors|url=http://www.fao.org/getinvolved/ambassadors/ambassadors/ambassadors-ritalevimontalcini/en/|publisher=FAO|access-date=31 December 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115090746/http://www.fao.org/getinvolved/ambassadors/ambassadors/ambassadors-ritalevimontalcini/en/|archive-date=15 January 2013}}</ref>\n\nIn 2001, she was nominated [[Senator for life|Senator-for-life]] by the Italian President [[Carlo Azeglio Ciampi]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Ghieth|first=Sheyam|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=aL4IesctLevA&refer=europe|title=Prodi May Need Elderly Senators to Keep Government|date=13 April 2006|publisher=Bloomberg}}</ref>\n\nIn 2006, Levi-Montalcini received the degree Honoris Causa in Biomedical Engineering from the [[Polytechnic University of Turin]], in her native city.\n\nIn 2008, she received the PhD Honoris Causa from the [[Complutense University of Madrid]], Spain.\n\nIn 2009, she received the Leonardo da Vinci Award from European Academy of Sciences.\n\nIn 2011, at the [[Sapienza University of Rome]] she received the PhD Honoris Causa from the [[McGill University]], Canada.\n\nShe was a founding member of [[Citt\u00e0 della Scienza]].<ref>{{cite web|title=E' scomparsa Rita Levi Montalcini, premio Nobel per la medicina, tra i soci fondatori di Citt\u00e0 della Academician of Studium, Accademia di Casale e del Monferrato, Italy.\n\n===Other attributions===\n* In April 2016, a spontaneous orchid (a hybrid between [[Ophrys incubacea]] and [[Ophrys sphegodes]]
