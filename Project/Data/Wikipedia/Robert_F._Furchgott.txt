He graduated with from the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] in 1937 with a degree in [[chemistry]] and went on to earn a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D]] in biochemistry at [[Northwestern University]] in 1940.<ref>[https://www.britannica.com/biography/Robert-F-Furchgott Robert F. Furchgott] at ''Encyclopedia Britannica''</ref>\n\n==Career==\n[[File:Clinton&1998NobelLaureates.jpg|thumb|left|upright=1.1|Furchgott and other 1998 [[Nobel Prize]] winners with former U.S. President [[Bill Clinton]], 25 November 1998]]\nFurchgott was faculty member and professor of [[pharmacology]] at [[Weill Cornell Medicine|Cornell University Medical College]] from 1940 to 1949, at [[Washington University School of Medicine]] from 1949 to 1956, at [[SUNY Downstate Medical Center|SUNY Brooklyn]] from 1956 to 1989, and at the [[University of Miami]] from 1989 through the end of his career.\n\nIn 1978, Furchgott discovered a substance in [[endothelium|endothelial cells]] that relaxes [[blood vessel]]s, calling it [[endothelium-derived relaxing factor]] (EDRF).<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Furchgott|first1=Robert F.|last2=Zawadzki|first2=John V.|date=November 1980|title=The obligatory role of endothelial cells in the relaxation of arterial smooth muscle by acetylcholine|url=http://www.nature.com/articles/288373a0|journal=Nature|language=en|volume=288|issue=5789|pages=373\u2013376|doi=10.1038/288373a0|pmid=6253831|bibcode=1980Natur.288..373F|s2cid=4303932|issn=0028-0836}}</ref> By 1986, he had worked out EDRF's nature and mechanism of action, and determined that EDRF was in fact [[nitric oxide]] (NO), an important compound in many aspects of cardiovascular physiology. Furchgott] at ''Encyclopedia Britannica''</ref>\n\n==Personal life==\nFurchgott was Jewish<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fau.edu/library/nobel34.htm |title=Seymour \"Sy\" Brody's |publisher=Fau.edu |access-date=2015-08-11}}</ref> and lived most of his married and career life in [[Woodmere, New York]] on [[Long Island]]. He was married to Lenore Mandelbaum (February 1915 &ndash; April 1983)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ssdi.rootsweb.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/ssdi.cgi |title=RootsWeb: Database Index |publisher=Ssdi.rootsweb.ancestry.com |access-date=2015-08-11}}</ref> from 1941 until her death at age 68. They had three daughters: Jane, Terry and Susan. His daughter, Susan, was an artist in the San Francisco counter-culture and co-founder of the [[Kerista|Kerista Commune]].\n\nFurchgott spent his later years with Margaret Gallagher Roth, who died March 14, 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9800E1DB143AF934A25750C0A9609C8B63 |title=Paid Notice - Deaths ROTH, MARGARET - Paid Death Notice - NYTimes.com |work=New York Times |date=2006-03-17 |access-date=2015-08-11}}</ref> He served as a professor emeritus at the [[State University of New York Downstate Medical Center]]. In 2008, he moved to [[Ravenna, Seattle|Seattle's Ravenna]] neighborhood.\n\n==Death==\nFurchgott died on May 19, 2009 in [[Seattle]].
