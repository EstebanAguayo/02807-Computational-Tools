(1996). ''A History of Swedish Literature''. University of Nebraska Press, p. 276.</ref> His poems and prose work are filled with a great joy of life, sometimes imbued with a love of [[History of Sweden|Swedish history]] and scenery, particularly its physical aspects.\n\n== Early life ==\n[[File:Olshammar huvudbyggnaden.JPG|thumb|Von Heidenstam's birthplace at Olshammar, now [[Olshammarsg\u00e5rden]]]]\nVerner von Heidenstam was born in [[Olshammar]], [[\u00d6rebro County]], on 6 July 1859 to a [[nobility|noble family]]. Von Heidenstam was the son of [[Gustaf von Heidenstam]], an engineer, and ''Magdalena'' Charlotta von Heidenstam ([[n\u00e9e]] R\u00fctterski\u00f6ld).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Von Heidenstam nr 2025 - Adelsvapen-Wiki|url=https://www.adelsvapen.com/genealogi/Von_Heidenstam_nr_2025|access-date=2021-10-10|website=www.adelsvapen.com}}</ref> He was educated at ''Beskowska skolan'' in [[Stockholm]].\n\nHe studied painting in the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Fine Arts|Academy of Stockholm]] but soon left because of ill health. He then traveled extensively in [[Europe]], [[Africa]] and the [[Orient]].<ref>Sohrabi, Bahram (2005). \"Early Swedish Travelers to Persia,\" ''Iranian Studies'' '''38''' (4), pp. 631\u2013660.</ref>\n\n== Literary career ==\nHe was at once greeted as a poet of promise on the publication of his first collection of poems,{{sfn|Hartmann|1920}} ''Vallfart och vandrings\u00e5r'' (''Pilgrimage: the Wander Years'', 1888). It is a collection of poems inspired by his experiences in the orient and marks an abandonment of naturalism that was dominant then in [[Swedish literature]].\n\nHis love for beauty is also shown by the long narrative poem ''Hans Alienus ''(1892). ''Dikter'' (\"Poems\", 1895) and ''Karolinerna'' (''The Charles Men'', 2 vols., 1897\u20131898), a series of historical portraits of King [[Charles XII of Sweden]] and his cavaliers, shows a strong nationalistic passion.<ref>[[Michelle Facos|Facos, Michelle]] (1998). ''Nationalism and the Nordic Imagination: Swedish Art of the 1890s''. University of California Press, p. 63.</ref><ref>Barton, H. Arnold (2002). \"The Silver Age of Swedish National Romanticism, 1905-1920,\" ''Scandinavian Studies'' '''74''' (4), pp. 505\u2013520.</ref> English translations of short stories from ''Karolinerna'' can be found in the ''American-Scandinavian Review'' (New York), May 1914, November 1915, and July 1916.{{sfn|Hartmann|1920}} The two volumes of ''Folkunga Tr\u00e4det'' (''The Tree of the Folkungs'', 1905\u201307) are the inspired, epic story of a clan of Swede chieftains in the Middle Ages.\n\nIn 1910 a controversy was waged in Swedish newspapers between a number of Swedish literary men on the topic of the proletarian \u201cdegradation\u201d of literature, the protagonists of the two opposing camps being [[August Strindberg]] and von Heidenstam. Professors Lidforss and B\u00f6\u00f6k also took part. von Heidenstam's chief contribution was the pamphlet, directed chiefly against Strindberg, \"Prolet\u00e4rfilosofiens uppl\u00f6sning och fall\" (\"The Decline and Fall of the Proletarian Philosophy\").<ref>Gustafson, Alrik (1940). \"Nationalism Reinterpeted: Verner von Heidenstam.\" In: ''Six Scandinavian Novelists''. New York: Biblo & Tannen, p. 169.</ref>\n\nvon Heidenstam's poetical collection ''Nya Dikter'', published in 1915, deals with philosophical themes, mainly concerning the elevation of man to a better humanity from solitude.\n\n== Personal life ==\n{{multiple image\n| direction = vertical\n| image1 = \u00d6vralid 002 Sweden.JPG\n| caption1 = Von Heidenstam's very last residence at [[\u00d6vralid]]\n| image2 = \u00d6vralids bibliotek.jpg\n| caption2 = \u00d6vralid's library\n}}\nHe died at his home [[\u00d6vralid]] on 20 May 1940.\n\n== Works ==\n* ''Fr\u00e5n Col di Tenda till Blocksberg '', pictures of travel (1888)\n* ''Vallfart och vandrings\u00e5r'' (1888)\n* ''Ren\u00e4ssans'' (1889)\n* ''Endymion'' (1889, novel)\n* ''Hans Alienus ''(1892)\n* ''Dikter'' (1895)\n* ''Karolinerna (''The Charles Men'', 1897\u201398, novel)\n* ''Sankt G\u00f6ran och draken ''(1900)\n* ''Klassizit\u00e4t und Germanismus'' (published in German, Vienna 1901)<ref>Here the author advocates a sort of artistic exclusiveness; Heidenstam appears as the champion of the classic spirit, which he considers essentially aristocratic, as opposed to the Germanic attitude which he considers democratic and reprehensible.</ref>\n* ''Heliga Birgittas pilgrimsf\u00e4rd'' (''Saint Bridget's Pilgrimage'', 1901)\n* ''Ett folk '' (1902)\n* ''Skogen susar ''(''The Forest Whispers'', 1904)\n* ''Folkungatr\u00e4det ''(''The Tree of the Folkungs'', 2 volumes, 1905\u20131907)\n* ''Svenskarna och deras h\u00f6vdingar'' (1910, historical lectures)\n* ''Nya Dikter'' (1915).\n\n'''Works in English translation'''\n* ''A King and his Campaigners'' (1902)\n* ''The Soothsayer'' (1919)\n* ''Sweden's Laureate.
