His research has focused on experiments that trap and [[Laser cooling|cool]] [[atom]]s to temperatures close to [[absolute zero]],<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://web.mit.edu/physics/people/faculty/ketterle_wolfgang.html|title = Wolfgang Ketterle|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = MIT Department of Physics}}</ref> and he led one of the first groups to realize [[Bose\u2013Einstein condensate|Bose\u2013Einstein condensation]] in these systems in 1995.<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.smithsonianmag.com/science-nature/the-coldest-place-in-the-universe-8121922/|title = The Coldest Place in the Universe|last = Shachtman|first = Tom|date = January 2008|work = Smithsonian Magazine|access-date = 27 October 2014}}</ref> For this achievement, as well as early fundamental studies of condensates, he was awarded the [[Nobel Prize in Physics]] in 2001, together with [[Eric Allin Cornell]] and [[Carl Wieman]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/2001/|title = Nobel Prize in Physics 2001|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = Nobelprize.org|publisher = Nobel Media}}</ref>\n\n==Biography==\nKetterle was born in [[Heidelberg]], [[Baden-W\u00fcrttemberg]], and attended school in [[Eppelheim]] and Heidelberg.<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite web|url = https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/2001/ketterle-bio.html|title = Wolfgang Ketterle - Biographical|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = Nobelprize.org|publisher = Nobel Media}}</ref> In 1976 he entered the [[University of Heidelberg]], before transferring to the [[Technical University of Munich]] two years later, where he gained the equivalent of his master's diploma in 1982.<ref name=\":0\" /><ref name=\":1\">{{Cite web|url = http://cua.mit.edu/ketterle_group/group_info/WK/CV%20WK.pdf|title = Curriculum Vitae - Wolfgang Ketterle|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = Alkali BEC Projects @ MIT|url-status = dead|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150426035255/http://cua.mit.edu/ketterle_group/group_info/WK/CV%20WK.pdf|archive-date = 26 April 2015}}</ref> In 1986 he earned a PhD in experimental [[molecular spectroscopy]] under the supervision of Herbert Walther and Hartmut Figger at the [[Max-Planck-Gesellschaft|Max Planck Institute]] for Quantum Optics in [[Garching]], before conducting postdoctoral research at Garching and the University of Heidelberg.<ref name=\":1\" /> In 1990 he joined the group of [[David E. Pritchard]] in the Research Laboratory of Electronics at MIT (RLE).<ref name=\":0\" />  He was appointed to the MIT physics faculty in 1993 and, since 1998, he has been [[John D. MacArthur]] Professor of Physics.<ref name=\":1\" />  In 2006, he was appointed Associate Director of RLE and began serving as director of MIT's Center for Ultracold Atoms.<ref name=\":1\" />\n\nAfter achieving [[Bose\u2013Einstein condensation]] in dilute gases in 1995, his group was in 1997 able to demonstrate [[Interference (wave propagation)|interference]] between two colliding condensates,<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://cua.mit.edu/ketterle_group/Projects_1997/Interference/Interference_BEC.htm|title = Interference of Two Condensates|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = Alkali BEC Projects @ MIT|url-status = dead|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160304092631/http://cua.mit.edu/ketterle_group/Projects_1997/Interference/Interference_BEC.htm|archive-date = 4 March 2016}}</ref> as well as the first realization of an \"[[atom laser]]\", the [[atom]]ic analogue of an optical [[laser]].<ref>{{Cite journal|url = [[molecule|molecular]] Bose condensate in 2003,<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/2003/ketterle.html|title = Physicists perform ultracold coup over molecules|date = 18 December runner, and was featured in the December 2009 issue of Runner's World's \"I'm a Runner\".<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.runnersworld.com/celebrity-runners/im-runner-wolfgang-ketterle-phd|title = to Stockholm when he received the Nobel Prize and happily running in the early dusk. Ketterle completed the 2013 Boston Marathon with a time of [[List of non-professional marathon runners|2:49:16]], <ref>{{Cite web|url = http://registration.baa.org/2013/cf/public/iframe_TopFinishers.htm|title = 2013 Boston Marathon Top Finishers|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = registration.baa.org}}</ref> and in 2014, in Boston, ran a personal record of 2:44:06.\n\nKetterle serves on the Board of Trustees of the [[Center for Excellence in Education]] (CEE),<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.cee.org/board-trustees|title = Board of Trustees|access-date = 27 October 2014|website = Center for Excellence in Education}}</ref> and participates in the Distinguished Lecture Series of CEE's flagship program for high-school students, the [[Research Science Institute]] (RSI), which Ketterle's own son Jonas attended in 2003. Ketterle sits on the International Scientific Advisory Committee of Australia's [[ARC Centre of Excellence in Future Low-Energy Electronics Technologies]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.fleet.org.au/team/?mgc_91=92/advisory-committees&mgi_91=776/wolfgang-ketterle|title = Prof Ketterle biography|access-date = 9 November 2017|website = Centre for Future Low-Energy Electronics Technologies}}</ref>\n\nKetterle is one of the 20 American recipients of the Nobel Prize in Physics to sign a letter addressed to President [[George W. Bush]] in May 2008, urging him to \"reverse the damage done to basic science research in the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations Bill\" by requesting additional emergency funding for the [[United States Department of Energy|Department of Energy]]\u2019s [[Office of Science]], the [[National Science Foundation]], and the [[National Institute of Standards and Technology]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=A Letter from America's Physics Nobel Laureates|url=https://fire.pppl.gov/nobel_bush_fy08_050808.pdf}}</ref>\n\n==Personal life==\nSince 2011, Ketterle has been married to Mich\u00e8le Plott.
